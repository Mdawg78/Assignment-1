Who celebrates it

The Lunar New Year is mostly celebrated in China and various countries across Southeast Asia. It is still celebrated in other parts of the world in Asian households and communities.

When it takes place

It takes place with the first new moon and it typically starts from the end of January until the full moon arrives.

What it signifies

It signifies good luck and prosperity for the coming year. It is also the time for families to reunite and pray for their ancestors.

How it is celebrated

The main performance of the Lunar New Year is the Lion Dance. Not just that, "People decorate their houses with red for good luck and children are given money in bright red envelopes."(Song & Howell, 2022).
